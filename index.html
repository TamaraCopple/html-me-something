Women in Tech Evangelist by Night

I have been the co-director for the for Kansas City Women in Technology's adult coding introduction, Coding & Cocktails since mid-2015. I attended the very first session of Coding & Cocktails in April 2015 at the encouragement of my husband; but it was the supportive and fun atmosphere, not to mention the raw enthuiastic energy of the program's organizers that hooked me. After that first session, I volunteered to help coordinate future programs and have not looked back since.

Tamara's Blog Post

Coding & Cocktails is about overcoming your inhibitions and self-doubt and trying something radically different. Despite the name, drinking alcohol is not required, but if a custom cocktail helps relax you enough to give it a try, we’re your show. If you discover programming is not your thing, that’s okay. Enjoy the drinks, enjoy the camaraderie, and pat yourself on the back for at least giving it a try. You’ve already done more than most.

. . . RPG Gamer in between!

Although I have played in Serenity, In Nomine, and Dresden RPG's Dungeons and Dragons is my default RPG of choice. I have played almost every character class at least once, run a 5-year homebrew campaign of my own creation, and am currently running the WOTC module Hoard of the Dragon Queen. Admittedly it's a compromise - as busy as the rest of life keeps me, I had to compromise somewhere and modules take the work of imagining a world, plot line and encounters and do it all for me so that I can concentrate on having as much fun running as my players do playing.
